Theorem 1.4.6. Properties of Continuity.

If \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) are continuous at \(x = c\text{:}\)

  1. \(f(x) + g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = c\)

  2. \(f(x) - g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = c\)

  3. \(f(x) \cdot g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = c\)

  4. \(f(x) / g(x)\) is continuous at \(x = c\) provided \(g(x) \neq 0\)

    (if \(g(x) = 0\) then \(f(x) / g(x)\) has a disconinuity)
