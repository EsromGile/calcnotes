Example 2.6.1.

Find the slope of the tangent to \(x^2 + y^2 = 1\text{,}\) at the point \(\left( \dfrac{1}{2},\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \right)\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} x^2 + (y(x))^2 \amp = 1 \amp \text{(treat y as y(x))}\\ \dfrac{d}{dx} \left(x^2 + (y(x))^2 \right) \amp = \dfrac{d}{dx}(1)\\ 2x + 2y \cdot \dfrac{dy}{dx} \amp = 0\\ 2y \cdot \dfrac{dy}{dx} \amp = -2x\\ \dfrac{dy}{dx} \amp = - \dfrac{x}{y} \end{align*}

At \(\left( \dfrac{1}{2},\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \right)\text{,}\) \(M_{\text{tan}} = \dfrac{-\frac{1}{2}}{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}} = -\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\)
