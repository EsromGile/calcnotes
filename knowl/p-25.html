Definition 1.1.5. Informal Definition of Limits.

If the values of \(f(x)\) can be make arbitrarily close to \(L\) by taking values of \(x\) sufficiently close to, but not equal to some number \(a\text{,}\) then we write:

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \end{equation*}

to mean either, "the limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x\) approaches \(a\) is \(L\)", or, "\(f(x)\) approaches \(L\) as \(x\) approaches \(a\)".
